# Case Study: The Marketplace UPI QR Code Scam

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**The Scenario:** Rohan, a university student, listed his old engineering drawing tools for sale on a popular online marketplace. Within minutes, a "buyer" contacted him, agreeing to the asking price without any negotiation. The buyer claimed they were out of town but wanted to lock in the purchase immediately by sending the money via Google Pay. 

**How it Happens (Step-by-Step):**
1. **The Hook:** The scammer creates a false sense of urgency, claiming they need to secure the item right away.
2. **The Trick:** Instead of sending money directly to Rohan's phone number, the scammer sends a QR code or a "Payment Request" link on WhatsApp. 
3. **The Manipulation:** The scammer calls Rohan and instructs him to open his UPI app, scan the QR code, and enter his UPI PIN, falsely claiming this step is required to "receive" the funds into his account.
4. **The Theft:** Confused by the urgency and the interface, Rohan enters his PIN. Instead of receiving the payment, the exact amount is instantly deducted from his own bank account and transferred to the scammer. The scammer then blocks Rohan's number.

**Who is Targeted:** College students selling used textbooks, laptops, or furniture are prime targets because they are often eager to make a quick sale and may be less suspicious of digital payment nuances. 

**Consequences:** The victim suffers an immediate, unrecoverable financial loss, which can be devastating on a tight student budget. It also causes immense stress and a lasting distrust of online peer-to-peer marketplaces.
