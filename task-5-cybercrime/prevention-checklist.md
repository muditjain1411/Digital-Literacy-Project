# Stay Safe Online: A Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for Students

✅ **1. Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** Your UPI PIN is strictly for *sending* funds or checking your balance. You never need a PIN to get paid.

✅ **2. Don't trust payment screenshots.** Scammers often use fake payment generator apps. Always check your actual bank SMS or your own UPI app's transaction history to confirm a payment went through.

✅ **3. Use strong, unique passwords.** Stop using the same password for your student portal, email, and social media. Use a password manager to keep track of complex passwords.

✅ **4. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).** Turn on 2FA for all critical accounts (Gmail, GitHub, LinkedIn, banking apps) to add an extra layer of security beyond just your password.

✅ **5. Be cautious on public Wi-Fi.** Never make financial transactions, pay tuition, or log into sensitive accounts while connected to unsecured cafe or campus Wi-Fi networks without using a reliable VPN.

✅ **6. Pause before you click.** Treat urgent emails or SMS messages with extreme suspicion—especially those claiming your account is blocked, a package is delayed, or you've won a lottery. 

✅ **7. Limit your digital footprint.** Avoid posting your location in real-time or sharing pictures of your university ID cards, flight boarding passes, or any document with a barcode/QR code.

✅ **8. Keep your software updated.** Regularly update your laptop's operating system, mobile OS, and apps to ensure you have the latest security patches installed.

---
### 🚨 **How to Report Cybercrime in India**
If you or a batchmate fall victim to an online fraud, act immediately:
* Block your bank accounts/cards instantly.
* Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at **1930**.
* File an official complaint online at the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)**.
