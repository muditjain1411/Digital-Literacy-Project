# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A quick checklist for maintaining a positive and professional digital footprint.

## ✅ The Do's (What you should do)
- **Do review your privacy settings:** Regularly check who can view your personal posts, photos, and location on platforms like Instagram and Facebook.
- **Do showcase your projects:** Use platforms like LinkedIn and Twitter to highlight your coding projects, hackathon wins, and academic achievements.
- **Do think before you post:** Always assume that future employers, internship coordinators, or professors might see your content.
- **Do engage respectfully:** Participate positively in online communities, tech forums, and university groups. 
- **Do verify information before sharing:** Fact-check news and announcements to avoid spreading misinformation or rumors among your batchmates.

## ❌ The Don'ts (What you should avoid)
- **Don't overshare personal details:** Never post photos of your university ID card, address, or phone number where anyone can see them.
- **Don't vent about professors or peers:** Avoid complaining about your university, specific classes, or classmates on public platforms. It leaves a permanent, negative impression.
- **Don't post inappropriate content:** Avoid sharing offensive, discriminatory, or unprofessional material that could compromise your academic or career opportunities.
- **Don't feed the trolls:** Avoid getting dragged into heated, unproductive online arguments or engaging in cyberbullying. 
- **Don't ignore your digital footprint:** Don't assume that deleting a post makes it disappear forever; screenshots last a lifetime.
